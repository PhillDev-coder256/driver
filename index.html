Free Asked Questions

Check out a list of frequently asked questions qabout our services.

  • 01 What is driver sobriety testing??

    Driver sobriety testing is a series of tests that are used to determine whether a driver is under the influence of alcohol or drugs. The most common type of driver sobriety test is the breathalyzer test, which measures the amount of alcohol in a driver's breath. Other types of driver sobriety tests include field sobriety tests, which assess a driver's balance, coordination, and reaction time.

  • 02 What are the different types of driver sobriety tests??

    The most common type of driver sobriety test is the breathalyzer test. Other types of driver sobriety tests include: Field sobriety tests: These tests assess a driver's balance, coordination, and reaction time. Some common field sobriety tests include the walk-and-turn test, the one-leg stand test, and the horizontal gaze nystagmus test. Drug tests: These tests can be used to detect the presence of drugs in a driver's system. Drug tests can be conducted using a variety of methods, including urine tests, blood tests, and saliva tests. Combination tests: These tests combine breathalyzer tests with field sobriety tests or drug tests.

  • 03What are the legal limits for driving under the influence of alcohol in my state?

    The legal limits for driving under the influence of alcohol vary from state to state. In general, the legal limit for blood alcohol concentration (BAC) is 0.08%. This means that a driver with a BAC of 0.08% or higher is considered to be under the influence of alcohol.
